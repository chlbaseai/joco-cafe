## About the Business
JO.CO Cafe is a Portuguese-inspired cafe in the heart of Handcross Village, West Sussex. Run by Michael, it's housed in a beautifully converted garage space called "The Forge Garage" and has become a beloved community staple over 5+ years of operation.

They describe themselves as "a dynamic cafe offering strong coffee, tasty treats and light lunches" with Portuguese roots. Their signature items — pastel de nata (Portuguese custard tarts) and homemade cinnamon buns — have earned a cult following. The cafe is known for its warm, welcoming atmosphere, Instagrammable presentation, and quality that rivals Portuguese cafes.

The business has recently expanded with a second location in nearby Balcombe, indicating growth and confidence. They also operate on Deliveroo for takeaway orders.

## Design Notes
- The cafe occupies a **converted garage** — this industrial-rustic backdrop is part of the charm. The website should nod to this without being "industrial" in the cold sense.
- Portuguese identity is key — think Lisbon tiles (azulejos), warm terracotta, pastéis, espresso culture. Subtle nods, not tourist clichés.
- "Instagrammable" was mentioned in reviews — the website should match this visual polish.
- They have **two locations** now — the site should accommodate both.
- **Deliveroo integration** — include a prominent "Order on Deliveroo" CTA.
- Community feel — "proper village feel cafe" — the site should feel personal and welcoming, not corporate.
